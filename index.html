Byzantine Generals Problem

Allegory developed to describe a situation in which, in order to avoid catastrophic failure of the system, the system's actors must agree on a concerted strategy, but some of these actors are unreliable.

Multiple generals besiege Byzantium. They've encircled the city, but they must decide when to assault as a group. They will win if all generals assault simultaneously; however, they will lose if they attack without coordination.
